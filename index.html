NARRATOR

The Clash of the Superheroes by Benjamin Woodrow-Fisch and Thomas Woodrow.

Long, long ago, there lived two superheroes at opposite ends of the galaxy.

One was named Transformatron. He lived in Sector Octaria Prime. He lived on Planet Kamo. While on his home planet, he had no powers, but only because the particular combination of rocks and minerals on his home planet kept him normal and average. As soon as he attained sufficient distance from Kamo, those rocks and minerals ceased to work on him, and his superpowers emerged. They were: shape-shifting, teleportation, mind control. He could control time, and he could create energy blasts and also invisible force fields.

On the other end of the galaxy lived his Imperial Majesty Hyperion Shrike, the 1958th, aka Hyperion. His home planet was called Regal by its inhabitants, Rock No. 247B by the rest of the universe, which was located in Sector 11. While on his home planet, he had no powers either. But only because the particular combination of rocks and minerals on his home world kept him normal and average. As soon as he attained sufficient distance from Rock two hundred forty seven B, those rocks and minerals ceased to work on him and his superpowers emerged. They were shapeshifting, super strength, laser beam eyes, and the ability to control metal. And he also had robot arms.

One day, on planet Earth, a cat was playing with a dangerous ball of yarn. The ball of yarn had been created by an evil mouse and would explode after being played with for a certain randomized period of time He never knew, not even the evil mouse, how long it would take before the ball of yarn and whatever happened to be moving it around, were summarily detonated in a spectacular ball of fire.

Well, both Transformatron and Hyperion, on their home planets, received messages about this impending tragedy, and both took it upon themselves immediately to intervene. So they both got on the spaceships that would convey them far enough from their respective homeworlds so their superpowers took effect, and they flew under their own superpowers from there.

As it happens, their superpowers were evenly matched, so evenly, in fact, that they arrived on the scene of impending destruction at exactly the same moment. So exactly the same was the moment of their arrival, and so complete was their focus on separating the cat from the Doom Yarn, that they neglected to notice that another superhero was also approaching at staggering speed.

Naturally, they bumped into each other, or, perhaps more accurately, smashed. The cat itself was so startled by the collision that it jumped off into space, never to be seen or heard from again. Legend tells of a successful beachside hotel owner on Planet Geet, whose enviable fate may or may not in fact have been that of this cat.

The ball of Doom Yarn was instantly vaporized before it could even begin to explode. A fight between the superheroes ensued. A fight… perhaps more accurately termed: a vast ever-spreading tapestry of imaginative destruction and devastation, and total disregard for human comfort.

TRANSFORMATRON

I am he who shall save this kitty!

HYPERION

No, it is I who am he who shall save this kitty.

NARRATOR

Hyperion sounded a little evil because even though he basically didn’t know any better, he had done some stuff that was fairly evil.

And anyway: BOFF! BAM! BOOM! They fought and fought and fought. Until at last they were tired.

Hyperion sat at the top of a building. Transformation sat in a park.

When all of a sudden, a little cart rolled up nearby, with pictures of lemons and other fruits on it.

TRANSFORMATRON

What is this?

NARRATOR

When the lady pushing the cart saw the alien superhero, she screamed and went running.

ITALIAN ICE LADY

It’s the monster who destroyed the city! Run! Help! Fire! Murder!

NARRATOR

But Transformatron was too intrigued to mind the babysitters rushing their children out of the park. He approached the mysterious cart. He opened it and saw inside large canisters of something. Immediately, he sensed danger and yanked one out of the cart, threw it into the sky and vaporized it with his Vape-o-matic. POFF!

And just then, Hyperion happened to look down and see what the other superhero was doing!

HYPERION

Dear god, he’s vaporizing something! Very likely a cat! He must be stopped!

NARRATOR

And Hyperion flew toward Transformatron at incredible speed, his fist extended in an unstoppable battering ram. And just before he struck Transformatron — which, catching him unawares, would have really done a number — a voice from somewhere said:

BENJAMIN

Hey!

NARRATOR

Well, Hyperion was so baffled that he stopped mid-flight and looked around for the source of the voice. For his part, Transformatron had had time to see that the other alien superhero was present in the park and was clearly distracted. So he readied his Vape-o-matic again…

BENJAMIN

Hey, no! Noooooo… Not on my watch!

NARRATOR

And the two superheroes looked down to see a little boy standing there, hands on his hips, chin jutted defiantly. They were dumbfounded. No one had ever… stopped them before. Not either of them.

BENJAMIN

Look, this…

NARRATOR

Benjamin went to the cart.

BENJAMIN

…this is Italian ice, okay? We do not vaporize it. We do not destroy it or melt it or send it to another dimension.

TRANSFORMATRON

Well, but then what do you do with it.

NARRATOR

Benjamin leaned into the cart, scooped out a lemon Italian ice into a cup, then another and another – three total – fished in his pocket for nine dollars and put them in the lady’s bucket.

BENJAMIN

You eat it!

NARRATOR

The superheroes looked at each other like they’d just heard the solution to faster-than-light travel. Or how to defeat the poisonous Twiggus on Planet Narfus. Or…

BENJAMIN

Here. Try it.

NARRATOR

And the superheroes each took their Italian ice, careful not to squash the little paper cups in their steel-clad super fingers.

And Hyperion tried it. And then Transformatron tried. And a light lit up their insides like nothing else had. And their stern and serious faces cracked into awkward smiles and they went silent and docile as, with the little wooden spoons, they finished off their Italian ices lickety split.

BENJAMIN

You don’t have any money, do you?

HYPERION

What is this “money” of which you speak.

BENJAMIN

You know, dollars. To pay for things. It’s okay, I got you this time, but next time you have to bring your own money, okay? They’re three dollars a piece.

NARRATOR

Hyperion advanced toward an old man nearby, hoisted him in the air, turned him upside down and began to shake him.

BENJAMIN

No, no, no! Put him down! Not someone else’s money. Your money that you earned.

NARRATOR

Well, now, this was a problem the superheroes had never confronted. Something they could not simply use their superpowers to take, as they had always deemed their right?

BENJAMIN

That’s how we do things on earth. We play fair with each other. It makes it much easier to be a human being and way more fun.

NARRATOR

At this, the superheroes nodded. They were exceptional, of course, and they did not have to play by these rules, but they were beginning to have a glimmer of understanding.

TRANSFORMATRON

We are all equal. All beings are equal.

BENJAMIN

Now you’re gettin’ it! Anyway, I gotta get to the playground – my dad’s waiting for me. But just remember: no more destroying stuff or fighting or taking stuff that’s not yours. Okay?

TRANSFORMATRON

Okay.

HYPERION^ Okay.

NARRATOR

And so the two superheroes flew back to their respective planets where they would no longer have their superpowers, but rather be the same as everyone else. And for the first time ever, they thought that at least until the next distress call, that might be okay.

For his part, Benjamin calmed down the Italian ice lady and explained about the superheroes. And went to find his dad at the playground.

BENJAMIN

Hey dad! I came up with a cool new game, want to play it?

THOMAS

Sure! How does it work?

BENJAMIN

You’re like this angry powerful superhero and I’m this angry powerful superhero and we meet on planet earth.

THOMAS

Is this based on a true story?

BENJAMIN

Maybe, yeah. But who cares? Let’s just pretend we’re superheroes and take it from there.

NARRATOR

And so they did. And went home soon thereafter to have dinner with Benjamin’s mom. And draw comics about Transformatron and Hyperion and their further adventures throughout the galaxy.

And then it was time for bed.
